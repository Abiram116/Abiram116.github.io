The story so far

Average on paper,
obsessed in practice.

I was never the studious kid. School and inter went by on average marks — but tech was always the thing. My dad's love for gadgets rubbed off early: I grew up fascinated by every new phone, tab, laptop and game I could get my hands on, taking the fun apart to see what made it tick.

B.Tech changed the gear. Studying AI & Data Science at KL University, I finally found the thing worth being obsessed about — and the marks followed the curiosity. These days that curiosity points at large language models: retrieval pipelines, agents that decide which tools to reach for, and interfaces that make all of it feel effortless.

The goal is simple: become the kind of AI engineer whose systems are as dependable as they are clever.

Two men, one obsession: the sky

Wings of Fire is my favorite biography of all time. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam's seaside Rameswaram years, the newspaper rounds, the slow burn toward Indian space research — I don't just admire that life, I daydream about it. And then there's Nambi Narayanan: the adventures he took on for ISRO and a dedication to the nation that's honestly astonishing. Rocketry, with Madhavan playing him, is my favorite biopic. These two are the reason "impossible" reads like a dare.

The garage of my dreams

I love cars — German ones especially, because the engineering under the sheet metal is a work of art. My soft spot is BMW's M division: the M4 is the evergreen, all-round favorite. And when the road ends, a Range Rover Defender is the only correct answer.
